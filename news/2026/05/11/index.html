🤖 OpenAI – OpenAI Rolls Out “ChatGPT Enterprise” with Unlimited GPT‑4 Turbo Access

New tier priced at $20 per user/month, offering unlimited GPT‑4 Turbo calls and higher data‑privacy guarantees. Integrated admin console, single‑sign‑on (SSO) and domain‑wide usage analytics for corporate teams. Early adopters include Salesforce, Shopify, and a consortium of Fortune 500 firms.

Why it matters: Enterprise‑grade access removes the usage caps that have limited large‑scale deployments, opening the door for AI‑driven workflows across finance, HR, and product development at a price point that scales with the workforce.

🟢 Google – Google DeepMind Announces Gemini 1.5: Multimodal Model with 1 Trillion Parameters

Gemini 1.5 combines text, image, audio, and video understanding in a single 1‑trillion‑parameter model. Benchmarks show 45 % improvement on VQA and 30 % on multilingual translation over Gemini 1.0. Available via Google Cloud Vertex AI with pay‑as‑you‑go pricing; early access program starts next week.

Why it matters: A truly multimodal LLM narrows the gap between specialist AI tools and a universal assistant, enabling developers to build apps that can “see, hear, and speak” without stitching together separate models.

🟣 Meta – Meta Launches “LLaMA‑3” Open‑Source Model, Now 70 B Parameters with Open Weights

LLaMA‑3 70 B released under the Meta Research License, allowing commercial use with attribution. Training data refreshed to include 2024 web content, improving factuality and reducing hallucinations. Accompanied by a new “LoRA‑Ready” toolkit for easy fine‑tuning on niche domains.

Why it matters: By providing a powerful, openly licensed model, Meta fuels the open‑source AI ecosystem, giving startups and academia a high‑performance alternative to closed APIs and accelerating innovation.

🟦 Microsoft – Microsoft Integrates Copilot into Teams Calls, Offering Real‑Time Summaries and Action Items

AI Copilot listens to live meetings, generating on‑the‑fly minutes, task lists, and follow‑up reminders. Supports 15 languages and can be toggled on/off per participant for privacy compliance. Rolled out to Microsoft 365 E5 customers today; broader availability slated for Q4 2024.

Why it matters: Embedding generative AI directly into collaboration tools reduces meeting fatigue and ensures that decisions are captured accurately, boosting productivity for remote and hybrid workforces.
